# Conclusion

## Key Takeaways
- Taiwan’s COVID-19 policies were significantly associated with case trends in 2021.
- Policy intensity increased during the May 2021 outbreak and was relaxed after cases declined.
- This pattern suggests that a **soft lockdown approach** was effective in Taiwan.
- In 2022, although policy data was less informative, the **mortality rate remained low**, suggesting improved epidemic control after vaccination expanded.

## Main Insight
The results suggest that government interventions can help slow the spread of COVID-19, even without maintaining strict long-term lockdowns.

## Limitations
- The dataset only reflects **Taiwan at the national level**, not regional differences.
- Policy levels are difficult to standardize across different social and cultural contexts.
- The literature review was limited, especially for Taiwan-specific policy research.
- The analysis mainly shows **association**, not direct causation.

## Future Improvement
If I continue this project, I would:
- compare Taiwan with other Asian countries
- include more policy and healthcare variables
- apply additional analytical methods
- improve chart design and visual clarity

## Final Summary
Overall, this project suggests that government policies were meaningfully related to COVID-19 case trends in Taiwan.  
The findings support the idea that **responsive, moderate restrictions** can still be effective when combined with strong public compliance and vaccination rollout.
